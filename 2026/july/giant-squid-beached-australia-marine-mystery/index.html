Colossal Giant Squid Washes Ashore in Australia, Baffling Marine Biologists

Liam Chen
Environment & Science Reporter
July 1, 2026
5 min read

Local residents and tourists on a remote stretch of Western Australia’s coastline were stunned Wednesday morning when they discovered a giant squid measuring more than 12 meters in length washed up on the sand.

The rare specimen, identified as Architeuthis dux, is one of the largest ever recorded in the Southern Hemisphere. Marine biologists from the Western Australian Museum and CSIRO arrived within hours to begin preservation and study.

Why Did It Beach?

Scientists are still investigating possible causes. While giant squid occasionally wash ashore, experts say the size and location of this specimen are highly unusual. Some theories include disorientation from naval sonar exercises, unusual ocean temperature shifts, or injury from deep-sea trawling equipment.

“This is an incredible opportunity to learn more about these elusive creatures,” said lead researcher Dr. Fiona Walsh. “Most of what we know comes from dead specimens or the stomachs of sperm whales. Having a relatively fresh animal of this size is rare.”

The squid will be transported to a specialized facility in Perth for detailed necropsy and eventual display at the museum.
