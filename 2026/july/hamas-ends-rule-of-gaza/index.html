Hamas Ends 20-Year Gaza Rule: Dissolves Government, Hands Power to New Technocratic Committee

Timor Lee
Editorial Team
July 8, 2026
6 min read

Hamas Dissolves Its Gaza Government After Nearly Two Decades of Rule

After nearly 20 years of governing the Gaza Strip since its 2007 takeover, Hamas announced on July 6, 2026, the dissolution of its Emergency Committee and the resignation of its head, Mohammed al-Farra. The group stated it has completed all administrative preparations to transfer governing authority to the National Committee for the Administration of Gaza (NCAG), a technocratic body. Civil servants and public employees will continue delivering services as usual, now under the responsibility of the new committee. Hamas spokesman Ismail al-Thawabta confirmed the resignation, while spokesman Hazem Qassem described the move as a step to remove pretexts for continued Israeli aggression and to facilitate the committee’s entry. Hamas has called on peace mediators and guarantor states to pressure Israel to allow the NCAG to enter Gaza and assume duties. The decision follows extensive internal discussions and was communicated to other Palestinian factions in Cairo, who reportedly welcomed it as a serious step toward enabling the new administration.

Technocratic NCAG Stands Ready but Insists on Unified Authority and Disarmament

The NCAG, headed by Palestinian technocrat and former Palestinian Authority official Ali Shaath, was established in January 2026 under the US-backed 20-point peace plan brokered by President Trump’s Board of Peace following the October 2025 ceasefire. The 15-member committee of non-partisan experts is tasked with managing civilian affairs including health, education, water services, humanitarian aid, reconstruction, and maintaining law and order through a unified police force. It operates under UN Security Council Resolution 2803 and reports to the Board of Peace and the UN. Shaath has declared the committee fully prepared to take over but stressed fundamental conditions: the existence of one single governing authority, one legal framework, and a unified security apparatus with all weapons consolidated under that authority. The Board of Peace has echoed this principle of “one authority, one law and one weapon,” requiring Hamas and other groups to disarm for the transition to succeed. The NCAG remains based in Cairo pending Israeli approval for entry.

Handover Remains Stalled as Israel Demands Full Disarmament and Hamas Awaits Roadmap Response

Despite Hamas completing transfer preparations and submitting its response to mediators’ revised roadmap, the actual handover has not occurred. Israel controls significant portions of Gaza and has dismissed the announcement as “spin without meaning” or a tactic to avoid disarmament obligations under the ceasefire’s second phase. Israeli officials insist on complete demilitarization of Hamas and all armed groups before any genuine power transfer or further withdrawals. The NCAG and Board of Peace have noted Hamas’s announcement but emphasized that progress will be judged by actions, not promises, and have called for enabling measures including Israeli withdrawal and unrestricted access. Reconstruction remains stalled amid ongoing disputes, with the committee unable to deploy inside Gaza. Hamas maintains it is ready to hand over security responsibilities as part of the transition while awaiting Israel’s reply on the roadmap. The move aligns with the broader US-mediated peace framework aimed at ending the conflict and enabling long-term governance reform, though deep divisions over disarmament and authority continue to delay implementation.
